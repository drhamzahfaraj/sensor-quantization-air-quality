\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related_work}

This section reviews five bodies of literature relevant to the VABQ framework: IoT-based air quality monitoring, sensor data reduction, neural network quantisation, edge AI for environmental sensing, and ADC energy modelling. The section concludes by synthesising the research gap that motivates this work.

\subsection{IoT-Based Air Quality Monitoring}
\label{sec:rw_aqm}

The deployment of low-cost sensor networks for indoor and outdoor air quality monitoring has accelerated in recent years. Karmakar et al.~(2024) developed the DALTON platform and conducted a three-month field study across 28 indoor sites, revealing that conventional indices such as the Indoor Air Quality Index (IAQI) fail to capture complex spatio-temporal pollution dynamics driven by occupant activities~\cite{karmakar2024exploring}. Their proposed Healthy Home Index (HHI) integrates average pollutant exposure, statistical variation, and spatial spread over a 10-minute sliding window. The same group later released an extended six-month dataset covering 30 sites with summer and winter seasons, accepted at the NeurIPS 2024 Datasets and Benchmarks Track~\cite{karmakar2024indoor}. Dimitriou et al.~(2025) surveyed innovations in air quality sensors and IoT integration, highlighting the growing role of low-cost particulate matter and gas sensors~\cite{dimitriou2025innovations}. Sheridan et al.~(2025) introduced Enviro-IoT, evaluating low-cost sensors over a 9-month field study and demonstrating that machine learning calibration substantially improves accuracy~\cite{enviroiot2025}.

Forecasting IAQ has also seen progress. Al-Saffar et al.~(2024) evaluated eight machine learning models for predicting indoor pollutants, finding that LightGBM achieved the best CO$_2$ prediction with MAPE of 0.096\%~\cite{forecasting2024iaq}. Chang et al.~(2023) proposed a LASSO and attention-TCN model for indoor particulate matter prediction~\cite{lassotcn2023}. Wang et al.~(2023) developed TCN-BiLSTM-DMAttention with STL decomposition for air pollutant prediction~\cite{tcnbilstm2023}. These temporal convolutional network (TCN) architectures confirm that temporal structure in pollutant signals can be effectively exploited for prediction and classification.

\subsection{Sensor Data Reduction and Compression}
\label{sec:rw_compression}

Reducing sensor data volume prior to transmission is a well-established strategy for conserving energy in wireless sensor networks. Atitallah et al.~(2023) proposed autonomous IoT data reduction based on adaptive thresholds with concept drift detection, achieving 11.7\% accuracy improvement at equivalent reduction rates~\cite{atitallah2023autonomous}. SAX-LZW two-level compression strategies~\cite{aljanabi2022sax} discretise continuous signals into symbolic representations before applying dictionary-based compression. Bounded-error pruning~\cite{azar2020bounded} selectively discards readings within a configurable tolerance band, achieving savings proportional to signal stationarity.

Recent work has explored reinforcement learning for adaptive data delivery. Pratama et al.~(2025) employed Q-learning to optimise transmission decisions for ESP32-based air quality nodes, achieving 40--50\% transmission frequency reduction~\cite{qlearning2025airquality}. Lopez et al.~(2023) provided a comprehensive vision for energy-sustainable IoT, identifying energy provision, transfer, and efficiency as three harmonious processes~\cite{energysustainable2023}. Ferraro et al.~(2024) investigated power consumption reduction for edge devices in ambient intelligence, demonstrating 2--3$\times$ operational lifetime extension~\cite{reducingpower2024}. However, all these methods operate at the raw signal or communication level without considering downstream inference model requirements.

\subsection{Neural Network Quantisation}
\label{sec:rw_quantisation}

Quantisation reduces numerical precision of neural network weights and activations, yielding proportional reductions in memory, computation, and energy. Nagel et al.~(2021) established theoretical foundations covering uniform and non-uniform quantisation, calibration, and bit-width--accuracy trade-offs~\cite{nagel2021whitepaper}. Gholami et al.~(2021) comprehensively surveyed PTQ, QAT, mixed-precision, and hardware-aware approaches~\cite{quantsurvey2021}.

PTQ methods require no retraining: ZeroQuant~(2022) introduced token-wise quantisation for transformers~\cite{zeroquant2022}; SmoothQuant~(2024) demonstrated training-free W8A8 quantisation through activation-weight smoothing~\cite{smoothquant2024}; QDrop~(2023) randomly dropped quantisation during calibration to improve extremely low-bit PTQ~\cite{qdrop2023}; and Q-HyViT~(2024) addressed PTQ for hybrid vision transformers on IoT systems~\cite{qhyvit2024}.

QAT methods incorporate quantisation into training for superior low-bit accuracy. EdgeQAT~(2024) introduced entropy-guided training for lightweight models on edge devices~\cite{edgeqat2024}. EfficientQAT~(2024) achieved state-of-the-art 4-bit accuracy with minimal loss~\cite{efficientqat2024}. HADQ-Net~(2025) presented hardware-adaptive QAT demonstrating that FP32-to-lower-bit quantisation reduces energy while maintaining accuracy~\cite{hadqnet2025}. Kim et al.~(2025) demonstrated accurate INT8 training through dynamic block-level fallback~\cite{accurateint8_2025}.

For mixed-precision quantisation, DyBit~(2023) proposed dynamic bit-precision numbers adapting to weight and activation statistics~\cite{dybit2023}. Dong et al.~(2024) reviewed mixed-precision methods showing that layer-wise precision allocation guided by sensitivity analysis yields optimal energy-accuracy trade-offs~\cite{dong2024efficient}. Guo et al.~(2024) introduced Edge-MPQ, a tightly integrated hardware-software co-design supporting INT2--INT16 precisions with 15.5--47.7$\times$ speedup over baseline RISC-V~\cite{edgempq2024}.

\subsection{Edge AI for Environmental Monitoring}
\label{sec:rw_edgeai}

The convergence of edge computing and AI has opened new possibilities for real-time environmental monitoring. Akhyar et al.~(2025) demonstrated TinyML-based ozone prediction on Arduino Nano with $R^2 = 0.95$, confirming that meaningful air quality inference is feasible on extremely constrained hardware~\cite{akhyar2025tinyml}. Kwon et al.~(2024) proposed TinyTrain for resource-aware sparse training at the data-scarce edge, achieving up to 1,098$\times$ memory savings~\cite{tinytrain2024}. Bauer et al.~(2025) introduced EdgeMLOps for operationalising ML models on edge devices, evaluating static and dynamic INT8 quantisation on Raspberry Pi~\cite{edgemlops2025}. Khan et al.~(2024) systematically evaluated quantisation for reducing CNN inference time on edge, confirming INT8 as the optimal precision for latency-accuracy balance~\cite{enhancingcnn2024}. Palakodety et al.~(2025) achieved 14.5$\times$ speedup with INT8 PTQ on a RISC-V NPU at only 1.96~W~\cite{palakodety2025tpu}.

Pandey et al.~(2025) introduced ML-guided resolution reconfiguration for neural recording, demonstrating 8.7--23$\times$ power savings by dynamically adjusting ADC resolution per channel based on decoder importance scores~\cite{pandey2025neural}. This work is conceptually closest to the sensor-level component of VABQ but targets a fundamentally different application domain (implanted neural interfaces) and does not address inference-level quantisation.

\subsection{ADC Energy Modelling}
\label{sec:rw_adc}

The relationship between ADC resolution and energy is characterised by the Walden FoM~\cite{waldenADC2018}:
\begin{equation}
    \text{FoM}_W = \frac{P_{\text{ADC}}}{2^{\text{ENOB}} \cdot f_s}
    \label{eq:walden}
\end{equation}
where $P_{\text{ADC}}$ is ADC power, ENOB is effective number of bits, and $f_s$ is sampling frequency. This implies power scales exponentially with resolution: $P_{\text{ADC}} = \text{FoM}_W \cdot 2^N \cdot f_s$. Andrulis et al.~(2024) developed an open-source ADC energy and area model confirming exponential energy growth with ENOB~\cite{andrulis2024adc}. State-of-the-art SAR ADCs achieve FoMs from 0.44~fJ/conv-step (11-bit, 90~nm)~\cite{waldenADC2018} to 16.6~fJ/conv-step (8-bit, 5~nm). Chen et al.~(2025) demonstrated a reconfigurable 2--8 bit SAR/SS ADC that eliminates unnecessary power at reduced resolutions~\cite{reconfigADC2025}. Dai et al.~(2021) showed that per-vector scaled quantisation further reduces energy in low-precision systems~\cite{vsquant2021}.

No prior work jointly optimises sensor-level ADC bit-width and inference-level model precision for air quality monitoring. Existing adaptive sampling methods use static or slowly adapting thresholds that fail to exploit the strong periodic structure of indoor pollutant data~\cite{karmakar2024exploring}. Resolution reconfiguration~\cite{pandey2025neural} targets neural interfaces, not environmental IoT. Edge-MPQ~\cite{edgempq2024} optimises inference hardware but not the sensing pipeline. The VABQ framework addresses this gap through a variance-entropy criterion governing both sensing and inference quantisation within a unified, mathematically grounded pipeline.
