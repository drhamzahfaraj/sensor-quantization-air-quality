\section{Discussion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\subsection{Practical Implications}

The VABQ framework is designed for direct deployment on the DALTON ESP32 platform without hardware modification. The rolling variance computation uses Welford's online algorithm, requiring only three floating-point variables per channel (count, mean, M2) with $O(1)$ per-sample update complexity. The entropy estimation uses a small histogram (16 bins) per channel, consuming negligible memory. The total memory overhead of the VABQ controller is approximately 256~bytes---well within the ESP32's 520~KB SRAM \cite{karmakar2024exploring}. The bit-width switching is implemented through the ESP32's configurable ADC attenuation and resolution settings, which support 9-bit to 12-bit hardware resolution; 4-bit and 16-bit modes are emulated through sub-sampling and oversampling respectively.

The 64.6\% total energy saving translates to a proportional extension of battery lifetime for portable deployments. For a 10,000~mAh battery bank powering the DALTON node at its baseline consumption of 3.55~W (760~mA at 5~V) \cite{karmakar2024exploring}, the operational duration increases from approximately 13.2~hours (full precision) to approximately 37.3~hours (VABQ), an extension of 2.83$\times$. For mains-powered deployments in multi-sensor buildings, the aggregate energy reduction across dozens of nodes yields meaningful sustainability benefits.

\subsection{Generalisability and Scalability}

While the experiments use the DALTON platform and indoor air quality data, the VABQ framework generalises to other sensor domains where signals exhibit temporal structure with alternating stable and transient periods. Outdoor air quality monitoring, water quality sensing, industrial emissions tracking, and environmental noise monitoring all share this characteristic. The variance-entropy criterion requires only that the signal have identifiable low-activity and high-activity regimes; the threshold parameters ($\theta_L$, $\theta_H$, $\alpha$) would need recalibration for each domain but the methodology is directly transferable.

Scalability to larger sensor networks is inherent in the per-node, per-channel design. Each sensor node runs the VABQ controller independently, with no inter-node communication required for bit-width decisions. The only centralised component is the inference model, which can be deployed identically across all nodes or tailored per-site if sufficient labelled data is available. For networks with hundreds of nodes, the aggregate energy savings scale linearly.

\subsection{Comparison with Hardware-Aware NAS Approaches}

An alternative methodology for energy-efficient edge AI involves hardware-aware neural architecture search (NAS) combined with dynamic quantisation and temporal convolutional networks (TCN). While NAS can discover highly optimised architectures, it entails an exponential search space, high computational cost (often requiring GPU clusters), non-deterministic results across runs, and difficulty in reproducing exact architectures. The VABQ approach achieves comparable or superior energy savings with a fundamentally simpler pipeline: the sensor-level controller is a deterministic threshold-based algorithm, and the inference-level quantisation is a standard PTQ procedure requiring only a small calibration set. This simplicity is a significant practical advantage for deployment in resource-constrained IoT environments where model updates must be infrequent and reproducible.

\subsection{Limitations and Future Work}

Several limitations warrant acknowledgement. First, the 4-bit emulation through sub-sampling introduces temporal aliasing that may miss very brief pollutant spikes (duration $<$ 10~seconds). Dedicated 4-bit ADC hardware would eliminate this limitation. Second, the current framework uses a fixed inference model; future work could integrate the sensor-level quantisation state into the inference model as a conditioning signal, enabling the model to adapt its internal representations to the current bit-width. Third, the entropy computation assumes stationarity within the 10-minute window, which may not hold during rapid environmental transitions. Adaptive window sizing based on change-point detection \cite{atitallah2023autonomous} could address this. Fourth, the experiments are conducted on indoor data from a single geographical region (India); validation across diverse climates, building types, and pollutant profiles would strengthen generalisability claims.
