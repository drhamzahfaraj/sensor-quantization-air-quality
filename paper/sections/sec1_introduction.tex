\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}

Indoor air pollution contributes to approximately 3.2 million deaths annually according to the World Health Organisation, making continuous monitoring of pollutants such as particulate matter (PM$_{2.5}$, PM$_{10}$), volatile organic compounds (VOCs), carbon dioxide (CO$_2$), carbon monoxide (CO), and nitrogen dioxide (NO$_2$) a pressing public health imperative \cite{karmakar2024exploring}. The proliferation of low-cost IoT sensor platforms has enabled fine-grained spatio-temporal mapping of indoor air quality in real time, yet these systems face a fundamental tension between monitoring fidelity and energy sustainability \cite{rodenas2022review, innovations2025sensors}. Higher sampling rates and greater ADC resolution improve the accuracy of pollutant characterisation but increase power draw at the sensor, data volume for transmission, and computational load for on-device inference.

Edge devices deployed for air quality monitoring---typically built around microcontrollers such as the ESP32---operate under stringent resource constraints. The ESP32 consumes approximately 45.5~mA (0.2275~W) in active mode and 5.27~mA (0.02635~W) in sleep mode when powered at 5~V \cite{esp32power2025}. With deep sleep, consumption falls to as low as 0.05~mA, enabling battery lifetimes exceeding 48 months under intermittent duty-cycling strategies \cite{benchmarking2022}. However, continuous monitoring at 1~Hz across multiple sensor channels demands sustained active-mode operation, rapidly depleting available energy budgets. The ADC is a significant contributor to this energy overhead; its power consumption scales exponentially with bit resolution according to the Walden figure of merit, where state-of-the-art SAR ADCs achieve figures of merit on the order of 0.44--16.6~fJ per conversion step \cite{waldenADC2018, andrulis2024adc}.

Neural network quantisation has emerged as a powerful technique for reducing inference energy on edge devices. Post-training quantisation (PTQ) compresses pre-trained model weights and activations from 32-bit floating point to lower-precision formats---typically INT8---without requiring expensive retraining \cite{zeroquant2022, smoothquant2024}. Recent work on SmoothQuant (2024) demonstrated training-free W8A8 quantisation that preserves accuracy through mathematically equivalent transformations that migrate quantisation difficulty from activations to weights \cite{smoothquant2024}. INT8 PTQ has been shown to deliver 14.5$\times$ speedup with only 1.96~W average power draw on edge hardware while maintaining high mean average precision \cite{palakodety2025tpu}. At the extreme, 4-bit fixed-point designs achieve 99.28\% accuracy with less than 0.3\% degradation relative to 32-bit baselines \cite{guo2020fixedpoint}.

Despite these advances, sensor-level quantisation and inference-level quantisation have largely been treated as independent problems. Sensor data compression methods---including adaptive thresholding \cite{atitallah2023autonomous}, bounded-error pruning \cite{azar2020bounded}, and SAX-based reduction \cite{aljanabi2022sax}---operate on raw signals without considering downstream inference requirements. Conversely, model compression techniques such as PTQ, quantisation-aware training (QAT), and mixed-precision quantisation \cite{dong2024efficient, hadqnet2025} optimise the neural network in isolation from the sensing pipeline. This separation leaves significant energy savings unrealised.

The DALTON platform provides an ideal testbed for investigating unified sensing-inference quantisation. Developed at IIT Kharagpur, DALTON is a custom-built IoT air quality monitoring system based on the ESP-WROOM-32 chip (dual-core Xtensa 32-bit LX6, 80--240~MHz, 448~KB ROM, 520~KB SRAM), equipped with research-grade sensors for PM$_1$/PM$_{2.5}$/PM$_{10}$ (DUST sensor, 0--500~$\mu$g/m$^3$, $\pm$10~$\mu$g/m$^3$ error), CO$_2$ (MH-Z16, 0--10000~ppm, $\pm$100~ppm + 6\%), and a multi-gas sensor (MCGS) measuring NO$_2$ (0.1--10~ppm), ethanol (1--500~ppm), VOC, and CO (5--5000~ppm) \cite{karmakar2024exploring}. Operating at a sampling frequency of 1~Hz with WiFi 2.4~GHz connectivity, the platform draws a maximum of 3.55~W (760~mA at 5~V). The three-month field study across 28 indoor sites---including households, studio apartments, research labs, canteens, and classrooms---generated a rich multivariate dataset with annotated occupant activities, providing ground truth for both signal reconstruction and activity-driven pollutant classification.

This paper makes the following contributions:
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
    \item A Variance-Aware Adaptive Bit-Width Quantisation (VABQ) framework that unifies sensor-level ADC resolution control with inference-level post-training quantisation in a single optimised pipeline.
    \item A variance-entropy criterion that dynamically selects between 4-bit, 8-bit, and 16-bit ADC resolution based on rolling signal statistics, exploiting the periodic structure of indoor pollutant data.
    \item Comprehensive experiments on the DALTON dataset demonstrating 61.3\% sensor-level and 74.8\% inference-level energy reductions with less than 3.9\% accuracy degradation.
    \item A comparative analysis against four baseline methods---uniform 16-bit, static 8-bit PTQ, fixed-threshold adaptive sampling, and SAX-LZW compression---validating the superiority of the dual-layer approach.
\end{enumerate}
