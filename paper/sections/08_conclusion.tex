\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

This paper introduces a novel hybrid framework for energy-efficient air quality monitoring in Internet of Things (IoT) deployments, integrating dynamic sensor-side quantization with hardware-aware temporal convolutional networks (TCNs) optimized for edge inference. Validated on the DALTON indoor air quality dataset—comprising 89.1 million samples from 30 geographically and socioeconomically diverse sites—the proposed approach demonstrates that aggressive quantization (4-6 bits per channel) coupled with temporal subsampling and residual encoding achieves up to 82\% energy reduction per sensor node while maintaining forecasting accuracy within 5-6\% of full-precision baselines.

\subsection{Key Contributions}

The primary contributions of this work are:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Adaptive dynamic quantization scheme}: We design a sensor-side quantizer with percentile-based range scaling updated every 300 seconds, exploiting pollutant variability to reduce quantization noise by 30-40\% compared to static ranges. Coupled with first-order residual encoding, this achieves 4-6 bit compression with minimal information loss.

\item \textbf{Quantization-aware TCN architecture}: We develop a lightweight temporal convolutional network with four dilated causal convolutional blocks (38,144 parameters, 42 KB INT8 model) that processes quantized inputs robustly via quantization-aware training. Ablation studies confirm 0.37 $\mu$g/m$^3$ RMSE improvement over post-hoc quantization, validating the importance of exposing models to quantization noise during training.

\item \textbf{Explicit energy-accuracy modeling}: We derive a hardware-aware energy model separating sensing, computation, and communication costs, quantifying that the proposed H-Q6$\Delta$ scheme reduces transmission energy from 12.8 to 2.45 $\mu$J/s (80.9\% savings) with RMSE degradation limited to 0.23 $\mu$g/m$^3$ (5.5\% relative increase).

\item \textbf{Comprehensive empirical validation}: Using 10-site chronological train-test splits spanning rural, suburban, and urban environments over 5 months, we demonstrate generalizability across seasonal variations (winter/summer), activity patterns (cooking/cleaning/quiescent), and diverse pollutant profiles.

\item \textbf{Ablation and sensitivity analysis}: Systematic experiments isolate contributions of adaptive range scaling (+0.32 $\mu$g/m$^3$), residual encoding (+0.22 $\mu$g/m$^3$), and quantization-aware training (+0.37 $\mu$g/m$^3$), establishing best practices for future quantization-aware IoT systems.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Significance and Impact}

This work addresses a critical gap in IoT environmental monitoring: the lack of integrated frameworks that jointly optimize sensor-side data compression and edge-based predictive inference. By demonstrating that 4-6 bit quantization maintains acceptable forecasting accuracy while enabling 80-82\% energy savings, we establish feasibility for deploying dense, battery-powered sensor networks in resource-constrained settings—including low- and middle-income communities disproportionately affected by indoor air pollution \cite{karmakar2024exploring}. The proposed methodology generalizes beyond air quality to water quality monitoring, smart building HVAC control, and other environmental sensing domains characterized by slow temporal dynamics and bounded signal ranges.

From a scientific perspective, this work advances the state-of-the-art in three dimensions: (i) task-aware quantization that exploits domain-specific signal characteristics (autocorrelation, bounded ranges), (ii) hardware-aware deep learning architectures (TCNs) optimized for quantized inputs and edge deployment, and (iii) explicit energy-accuracy trade-off modeling enabling principled system design. These contributions provide a blueprint for co-designing sensing, communication, and inference layers in energy-constrained IoT applications.

\subsection{Practical Deployment Guidelines}

Based on experimental findings, we recommend the following guidelines for practitioners deploying quantization-aware air quality monitoring systems:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Quantization bit-width}: Use 6 bits per channel (H-Q6$\Delta$) for balanced accuracy-energy trade-offs, or 4 bits (H-Q4$\Delta$) when energy constraints are paramount and 10-15\% RMSE degradation is acceptable.

\item \textbf{Adaptive range updates}: Update quantization ranges every 5 minutes ($T_R = 300$ s) using 1st and 99th percentiles of recent observations. Shorter intervals ($T_R < 150$ s) offer diminishing returns while increasing metadata overhead.

\item \textbf{Temporal subsampling}: Transmit residuals every 4 seconds ($T_S = 4$ s) for 10-minute forecasting horizons. Longer intervals ($T_S > 8$ s) risk undersampling transient events; shorter intervals ($T_S < 2$ s) yield marginal accuracy gains with higher energy costs.

\item \textbf{Reference resets}: Transmit absolute reference values every 60 seconds ($N_{\text{ref}} = 15$ packets at $T_S = 4$ s) to prevent drift accumulation in residual chains.

\item \textbf{Model training}: Always employ quantization-aware training (QAT) with simulated quantization operators in the training loop. Post-hoc quantization causes 7-10\% accuracy degradation.

\item \textbf{Hardware selection}: Prioritize low-power wireless transceivers (BLE, Zigbee) with $E_{\text{tx}} < 100$ nJ/bit. For LoRaWAN deployments, increase $T_S$ to 40-60 s to comply with duty-cycle regulations.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Future Directions}

Future work will explore several promising extensions:

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Hardware-aware neural architecture search}: Automate co-optimization of TCN architecture (dilations, channels, kernels) and quantization bit-widths using multi-objective evolutionary algorithms balancing accuracy, latency, and energy on target edge devices.

\item \textbf{Federated learning with quantized gradients}: Enable privacy-preserving collaborative training across distributed sensor nodes by combining sensor-side quantization with gradient compression, targeting $<$1 KB/round communication overhead.

\item \textbf{Multi-pollutant joint forecasting}: Extend the TCN to multi-task learning architectures predicting PM$_{2.5}$, PM$_{10}$, CO$_2$, and VOC simultaneously, with mixed-precision bit allocation prioritizing high-importance pollutants.

\item \textbf{Outdoor and water quality validation}: Rigorously evaluate the framework on outdoor urban air quality datasets (e.g., Beijing, Los Angeles) and aquatic sensor networks, adapting quantization ranges and residual scaling to broader dynamic ranges.

\item \textbf{Real-world deployment study}: Conduct longitudinal field trials (6-12 months) with battery-powered sensor nodes in residential buildings, quantifying battery lifetime extension, maintenance costs, and user acceptance.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Closing Remarks}

As IoT sensor networks proliferate for environmental monitoring, health surveillance, and smart city applications, energy efficiency and communication overhead emerge as critical bottlenecks constraining scalability and deployment feasibility. This paper demonstrates that principled co-design of sensor-side quantization and edge-based deep learning enables order-of-magnitude energy savings without sacrificing predictive performance, paving the way for sustainable, dense sensor infrastructures in resource-constrained settings. By establishing a rigorous methodology grounded in real-world data (DALTON), explicit energy modeling, and comprehensive ablation studies, we provide both scientific insights and practical tools for researchers and practitioners advancing next-generation IoT environmental sensing systems.

The proposed hybrid quantization-TCN framework, validated across diverse indoor environments and activity patterns, establishes that 4-6 bit sensor quantization is not merely a compromise necessitated by energy constraints, but rather an effective compression strategy that preserves essential temporal structure for accurate forecasting. As edge AI hardware continues to improve (e.g., neural processing units, quantized accelerators) and low-power communication protocols mature (e.g., BLE 5.3, Wi-Fi HaLow), the integration of sensor-level and model-level optimization will become increasingly critical for realizing the vision of ubiquitous, sustainable environmental monitoring at global scale.
