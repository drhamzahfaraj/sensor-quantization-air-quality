\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

This paper presented the Variance-Aware Adaptive Bit-Width Quantisation (VABQ) framework for energy-efficient air quality monitoring on edge IoT devices. By jointly optimising sensor-level ADC resolution and inference-level model precision through a unified variance-entropy criterion, VABQ achieves a 64.6\% reduction in total system energy while maintaining classification accuracy above 96.1\% and reconstruction NRMSE below 2.4\%.

Experiments on the DALTON dataset---spanning 30 indoor sites, 46 occupants, and six months of continuous multi-channel data at 1~Hz~\cite{karmakar2024exploring, karmakar2024indoor}---demonstrate practical viability on ESP32-based hardware with negligible memory overhead. The adaptive bit-width allocation, governed by the variance-entropy criterion (Equation~\ref{eq:gamma}), assigns 4-bit resolution during 59.5\% of measurement time, concentrating full 16-bit resolution on the 13.2\% of time windows containing activity-driven transients.

The dual-layer design bridges sensor data compression~\cite{atitallah2023autonomous, azar2020bounded} and neural network quantisation~\cite{nagel2021whitepaper, smoothquant2024}, constituting the principal novelty. Comparative analysis against four baselines and eight published methods confirms that joint optimisation yields compound savings exceeding either layer alone. The deterministic, threshold-based sensor controller combined with standard INT8 PTQ ensures reproducibility and ease of deployment---significant advantages over NAS-based or RL-based alternatives~\cite{qlearning2025airquality, edgempq2024}.

Future work will extend VABQ to outdoor networks with heterogeneous sensor modalities~\cite{enviroiot2025, dimitriou2025innovations}, integrate adaptive window sizing~\cite{atitallah2023autonomous}, and explore end-to-end quantisation-aware training across both layers~\cite{edgeqat2024, efficientqat2024}. Dedicated reconfigurable ADC hardware~\cite{reconfigADC2025} and mixed-precision inference units~\cite{edgempq2024} represent promising avenues for further energy reduction beyond software-level optimisation.
