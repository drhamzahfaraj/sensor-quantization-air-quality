\section{Results and Analysis}
\label{sec:results}

\subsection{Temporal Signal Characteristics}
\label{sec:res_temporal}

Analysis of the DALTON dataset confirms the strong periodic structure that VABQ exploits. Kitchen VOC measurements exhibit sharp transient peaks exceeding 800~ppb during cooking, with rapid decay under ventilation but prolonged lingering (hours) in poorly ventilated conditions~\cite{karmakar2024exploring}. Bedroom CO$_2$ accumulates overnight during sleep, reaching median levels above 1,200~ppm with closed windows and recirculated air~\cite{karmakar2024indoor}. Temperature follows steady diurnal trends with low variance outside of ventilation changes. Humidity correlates with cooking and seasonal transitions.

Across all sites and channels, signals remain in the low-variance regime ($\Gamma_c < \theta_L = 0.15$) for 62.7\% of measurement time. The medium-variance regime ($\theta_L \leq \Gamma_c < \theta_H = 0.55$) accounts for 24.1\%, while the high-variance regime ($\Gamma_c \geq \theta_H$) occupies only 13.2\%. This distribution is highly favourable for energy saving, as the majority of time is spent at 4-bit resolution.

VABQ achieves 96.1\% accuracy---a 1.6 percentage point reduction from B1. \subsection{Sensitivity Analysis}
\label{sec:res_sensitivity}

\subsubsection{Effect of Window Size $\tau$}

Shorter windows ($\tau = 2$~min) respond quickly to transients but produce noisy variance estimates, increasing bit-width switching overhead. Longer windows ($\tau = 30$~min) provide stability but miss brief events. The optimum $\tau = 10$~min, consistent with the DALTON HHI formulation~\cite{karmakar2024exploring}, achieves the lowest combined distortion-energy metric.

\subsubsection{Effect of Weighting Parameter $\alpha$}

Varying $\alpha$ from 0 (entropy-only) to 1 (variance-only) reveals that $\alpha = 0.65$ minimises the Lagrangian. Pure variance ($\alpha = 1$) misclassifies noisy-but-stationary signals as dynamic, over-allocating bits. Pure entropy ($\alpha = 0$) under-responds to magnitude changes in slowly drifting signals. The combination captures both amplitude variation and information content.

\subsubsection{Effect of Threshold Values}

Varying $\theta_L \in [0.05, 0.30]$ and $\theta_H \in [0.35, 0.75]$ reveals a Pareto frontier between energy saving and fidelity. The selected values ($\theta_L = 0.15$, $\theta_H = 0.55$) lie on the knee of this frontier. Aggressive thresholds ($\theta_L = 0.25$, $\theta_H = 0.70$) increase 4-bit allocation to 72.4\% and energy savings to 71.2\%, but NRMSE rises to 3.8\% and accuracy drops to 93.7\%.
