\begin{abstract}
Low-cost Internet of Things (IoT) air quality monitoring systems face a critical trade-off between temporal resolution, measurement accuracy, and energy consumption. This paper proposes a novel hybrid scheme that integrates sensor-side dynamic quantization with hardware-aware temporal convolutional networks (TCNs) to optimize energy efficiency while maintaining prediction accuracy. Using the DALTON indoor air quality dataset comprising 89.1 million samples from 30 sites, we demonstrate that 4-6 bit quantization coupled with temporal subsampling and residual encoding achieves up to 82\% energy reduction compared to full-precision transmission, with minimal degradation in forecasting performance (RMSE increase of only 0.5 $\mu$g/m$^3$ for PM$_{2.5}$ prediction). Our methodology combines dynamic range adaptation every 300 seconds, delta coding with 4-second intervals, and an INT8-quantized TCN deployed at edge gateways. Experimental results across diverse indoor environments validate the scheme's generalizability and practical viability for battery-powered, dense sensor deployments in resource-constrained settings.
\end{abstract}
